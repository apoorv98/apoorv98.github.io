Hi, I'm Yaoyu Yang. I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical Engineering at University of Washington, working on synthetic biology and lab automation in the Klavins Lab. Currenlty, I also work as an engineer in a startup called Aquarium to build a 21st century laboratory operating system.

I studied automation in my undergraduate at Shanghai Jiao Tong University, intersted in control theory, and its applications in electronic and electrical systems. I began my graduate study in UW EE in fall 2011 and found myself fascinated by the emerging area of synthetic biology where I could apply my knowledge in automation to engineer biological systems, which could potentially lead to disruptive innovations in biotechnology. I joined Klavins Lab since then and have been working on several research projects in synthetic biology with the aim of making designing, building and characterizing engineered biological system easier and higher throughput. I also worked greatly on building high quality, reproducible cloning workflows in the Aquarium lab oprating system we invented in the Klavins Lab and currently help building commercial solutions at Aquarium.

Outside lab and work, I enjoy reading, making cool apps, and helping with my wife's bridal startup.
